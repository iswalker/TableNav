Why TableNav

Everything you need.
Nothing you don't.

Built for analysts who live in data every day and can't afford to fight their tools.

Pivot Without the Guesswork

Field previews show sample values right in the field list — so you always know which column you're working with before you pivot it. No more toggling back and forth to remember what a column contains.

Filter at the Speed of Thought

Hover any column header and press Space to open a live filter pane — no clicks, no menus. Date columns automatically group by year, month, and day. Numeric columns get a "greater than" threshold. Hit Esc to clear everything instantly.

Filter Directly Into the Data

No more maintaining 10 pivot tabs in a workbook. Double-click any value in the Inspect panel to filter the source grid instantly. Close the panel and you're back in the filtered data — no intermediate steps.

Pure Data — Zero Noise

TableNav strips away every font, color, border, and conditional-format decoration that accumulates in shared Excel files. What's left is your actual data, in a consistent grid you can read at a glance.

Instant Launch

TableNav starts faster than Excel opens its splash screen. It's a single .exe — no installer, no runtime download prompt, no loading bar. Double-click and your data is there.

Columns That Fit Themselves

Column widths are calculated automatically from your data on every load, edit, and paste. The widest value wins — up to a sensible cap — so you always see as much data as possible without manual dragging.

Median as a First-Class Stat

Select any range of cells and the status bar shows sum, count, average, and median — no formula required. Median is the stat analysts actually need and Excel buries behind a function call.

Multiple Files, One Window

Open as many CSV or XLSX files as you need in a single TableNav window. Switch between them with keyboard shortcuts, and each tab remembers its own filters, pivot state, and scroll position independently.

Inline Color Previews

When a cell contains a hex color code like #3a86ff, TableNav renders a small color swatch right in the cell. Store design tokens, brand palettes, or status codes cleanly — and still see what you're working with.
